DEVELOPMENT AND CHARACTERIZATION OF INNOVATIVE PLATFORMS BASED ON THE TECHNOLOGY OF MICROFLUID PAPER DEVICES (ÂµPADs) WITH CHEMICALLY MODIFIED SURFACE FOR DETECTION OF SUBSTANCES OF INTEREST

The operating principles of sensors most often include complicated manufacturing technology, sophisticated measuring equipment, sensitive materials and high production costs, making their commercialization problematic. Therefore, there is a need to search for new materials that can be used as devices for the manufacture of new sensors and new techniques to overcome these problems. The use of portable, simple, accurate and inexpensive analysis methods is necessary to carry out environmental monitoring in such a way that samples can be analyzed in the field, to investigate and obtain knowledge of natural processes, as well as to analyze chemical species existing in the environment under study. Among the devices recently introduced in analysis laboratories, the cheapest are paper ones. They are easy to use, require small reagent and sample volumes, provide rapid analysis, are disposable, and can be made from renewable substrate materials. The development of these devices has become a goal in analytical science for several decades and the improvement of sensors has benefited from the emergence of advances in areas such as optical technology, chemometrics, enzyme immobilization chemistry, ultramicroelectrode design, among others. Biological fluid and environmental analyzes are necessary to monitor population health, but these measures are difficult to implement in remote regions, such as those found in less-industrialized countries, or in emergency situations. Conventional laboratory instruments provide quantitative measurements of biological samples, but are inadequate for these situations, as analyzes are often expensive and require trained personnel and considerable sample volumes. Microfluidic devices have been used in a variety of areas, including bioanalysis, pharmaceuticals, forensic and clinical applications, as a practical example we have commercially available paper strip tests for pregnancy, diabetes, drugs, etc., in addition to pH indicator paper, which It is cheap, does not require any type of equipment or signal amplification system, is highly sensitive and has a long lifespan. Therefore, it is believed that paper devices can be one of the cheapest platforms available for developing assays, as they are portable and technically simple to detect a number of important species.

BIOSYNTHESIS OF METAL NANOPARTICLES USING BIO EXTRATUS AND DEVELOPMENT OF ANTIBACTERIAL COTTON FIBER

The unique, new and differentiated properties of nanomaterials have attracted the attention not only of scientists and research centers, but also of companies, due to their enormous economic potential. Nanotechnology has been applied in several areas, such as medical devices, communication technologies and biotechnology. Using the principles of green synthesis in conjunction with nanotechnology provides the ability to minimize the possible impacts of these systems on human health and the environment, through synthetic mechanisms that provide the creation of new materials without the use of strong reducing agents, polymers, solvents, surfactants, etc., and contributing to the reduction of toxic waste generated. Biological synthesis methods - green chemistry - of nanoparticles use microorganisms such as fungi, enzymes, cellulose, chitosan, honey, plant and vegetable extracts, with numerous compounds that have the potential to form and stabilize metallic nanoparticles. The reduction of metal ions by sets of molecules found in extracts such as enzymes, proteins, ammonia, polysaccharides and vitamins is chemically benign, resulting in an enormous volume of successful research using these compounds. The application of nanotechnology in the textile industry is still very recent. One of the objectives of using nanotechnology in the textile industry is to improve functionalities and/or incorporate new characteristics into fibers, threads and fabrics. Through nanometric materials, manufacturers can give new properties to fabrics, thus giving them new functionality. The properties conferred on textile products that use nanotechnology include water and oil repellency, wrinkle resistance, moisturizing agents, deodorants, insect repellents, anti-tobacco, anti-humidity, antibacterial, anti-static, flame retardant agents, among others. In addition to these characteristics, it can also provide high durability for tissues, as nanoparticles have a huge area/volume ratio and high surface energy. Thus, they have better affinity for tissues and provide an increase in the durability of their functionality. The addition of new functionalities to cotton fabrics through nanotechnology is an important contribution to adding value, as it allows the incorporation of characteristics into the cotton fiber without losing its main values.

SYNTHESIS OF COLEOPTERA AGGREGATION PHEROMONES OF ECONOMIC INTEREST

The search for techniques that are less harmful to the environment to control insect pests of economically important crops has motivated several research groups to work on this topic. The use of pheromones has stood out in this sense, as they are highly specific substances, that is, they only act on one species. The technology based on the use of these substances is based on the premise that we must develop natural devices that help control insect pests in crops of economic interest and that can consequently reduce the use of chemical pesticides in plantations as much as possible. In this context, this research project aims to develop new synthesis routes for the aggregation pheromones of the insects Cosmopolites sordidus and "Rhynchophorus palmarum" which can be used in Integrated Pest Management (IPM) in the Brazilian agricultural production system.
